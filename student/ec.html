Mapping Class and Language in The Great Gatsby

Eric Castner | DHUM 204

This project takes a deep dive into The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald. This will take shape around a guiding question: How do patterns of language and geography in The Great Gatsby work together to reveal and reinforce structures of wealth, class, and social division? Fitzgerald creates a class structure through not only plot and character development, but also by recurring word patterns and spatial organization, and more specifically, physical division, throughout the novel. Time functions as a secondary element that reinforces the same themes. Voyant Tools is used to move deeper into these worlds, past general observations. More specifically, it will provide data of word frequency, character prominence, word associations, and trends over the course of the novel to show how specific words relate to certain characters, themes, and social groups. Google My Maps is also utilized to map out how Fitzgerald's development of space reinforces the theme of class and wealth disparity. Overall, this project provides viewers with a deeper description that contains clear evidence to support a central argument, which ultimately shows how digital humanities tools can reveal deeper meanings throughout literature. Through connecting language, space, and time, it is clear that Fitzgerald constructed a social world to provide commentary on themes that go deeper than the surface of the novel.
