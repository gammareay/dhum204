Guide/Template on Setting Up HTML

Nicholas Okken | DHUM 204

Feel free to copy/paste any of this to help with your pages!

Tags are represented by < > with a keyword or letter inside. They specify how HTML should be rendered. You can use bold and italic text by using < > with the letter b inside for bold or i for italics. You close < > by repeating the same tag, but with a / before the letter.

Using p in the tag lets you create individual paragraphs. Additionally, enter is NOT rendered as a line break. Instead, use br in your < > tag. No closing tag necessary!

Here, you can embed an image. First, it must be uploaded to the repository. Then, copy the text below in the this file's raw HTML and change book-2.png to the actual filename of your image. Update the alt text as well, and add a visible caption.

Subheadings

To set a subheading, use h3 in your < > tag. This makes it follow the predefined header formatting.

To embed other content on your page, such as a Google MyMaps map, copy the text below from the source HTML file, and replace the URL with the one for your page. Alternatively, MyMaps will provide an embed to directly copy/paste into your file by clicking the three vertical dots near the title of your map and selecting "Embed on my site".
