Static site development has been an integral part of the minimal computing community and an approach used by Digital Humanities practitioners to not only build sustainable, portable, and archivable digital projects, but increasingly emphasises an ethically-minded position towards web development. Throughout the semester, the class will work towards building a website together that features select excerpts or reflections from mini projects or readings. The goal of this work is to experiment with collaboratively making a website that is as light weight and as possible, while also equipping students with some fundamental understanding of foundational web-building practices.
